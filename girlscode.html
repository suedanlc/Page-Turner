Why Do We Need Girls To Code

The Problem

Computing skills are the most sought after in the US job market. Computing is where the jobs are -and where they will be in the future. -

Yet research shows that the share of woman in the computing workforce has declined from 37% in 1995, to 24% today and will continue to fall to 22% by 2025

This shortage is a fundamental economic challange for the US economy, and greatly effects its ability to compete globally.

Our Mission

Girls Who Code works to inspire, educate, equip girls with computing skills to persue 21st century opportunities.

Our vision is to reach gender parity in computing and technology sectors.

We built a supportive sisterhood of peers and role models who help our students and alumni persist and succeed.

Our programs create clear pathways for girls who code alumni from middle and high school into the computing workforce.
